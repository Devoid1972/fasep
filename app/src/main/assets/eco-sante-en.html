This map shows the health of local environments, using three indicators :

Water management

For each hexagonal tile, the water-related risks (such as flood, water quality and quantity) are calculated from 0 to 10.

Protected areas

Each tile is rated according to its level of protection. The stronger the protection, the better the rating is. If an area combines different sources of protection (local, national or international), its rating is also higher.

Urbanisation

Here, urban densities are calculated from 0 to 10 for each tile. 10 represents a wild and unspoiled land, whereas 0 indicates a fully urbanized area.

We then calculate the average of those three indicators for each tile, in order to eventually plot the eco-health level of the area that it represents. Note that each hexagonâ€™s area is 20 square km.
